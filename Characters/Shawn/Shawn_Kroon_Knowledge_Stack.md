# Alberta Locations : Scene Director Reference

**Knowledge Stack for the Scene Director persona. Real-world geography for grounding narrated scenes.**

## The Property (Foothills County, Near Priddis)

The property sits in the foothills of the Canadian Rockies, elevation roughly 1,250-1,350 metres. The landscape is rolling grassland transitioning to aspen and spruce forest. Views west to the front ranges of the Rockies. The Cowboy Trail (Highway 22) runs nearby. The area is ranching country: cattle on neighboring properties, barbed wire fencing, hay bales in fall.

### Seasonal Conditions

- **Winter (November-March).** Snow cover is variable due to chinook winds. Temperature swings of 20-30°C in 24 hours are possible when a chinook arch (a distinctive band of cloud along the mountain ridge) forms. Clear nights hit -25 to -35°C. The sky is black and the Milky Way has visible structure in the dark sky preserve. Snow squeaks underfoot. Breath hangs as fog. Metal is painful to touch bare-handed.
- **Spring (April-May).** Mud season. Snow melts unevenly. Creeks and rivers run high from snowmelt. The first green appears on south-facing slopes. Canada geese return. Calving season on the neighboring ranches. Rain and wet snow alternate. Roads can be greasy.
- **Summer (June-August).** Long days (sunrise before 5:30 AM, sunset after 9:30 PM in June). Wildfire smoke from BC can haze the sky orange for weeks in late July and August. Thunderstorms build fast over the mountains and roll east across the foothills in the afternoon. The air smells like warm grass and sage. Temperatures reach 28-32°C. Horseflies near water.
- **Fall (September-October).** Aspen turn gold on the hillsides. First frost as early as late August in low spots. Larch turn amber higher up. The light goes low and amber by mid-afternoon. Elk bugling can be heard from the ASCCA. The air is dry and clean after the smoke clears.

### Property Sensory Notes

The property is in a dark sky preserve zone. On clear nights, the Milky Way is visible as a luminous band with dark dust lanes. The Rothney Astrophysical Observatory dome is visible as a silhouette on the ridge to the south. Cattle from the neighboring property can be heard, a low sound that carries on still evenings. Coyotes at dusk. The creek on the property runs higher in spring and fall, lower in late summer. The wind is predominantly from the west or southwest, carrying mountain air.

## Ann & Sandy Cross Conservation Area (ASCCA)

Located immediately adjacent to the property. 4,800 acres of protected foothills grassland, aspen groves, and mixed forest. Managed by the Ann & Sandy Cross Conservation Foundation. Trail access directly from the east paddock of the property.

### Terrain

Rolling foothills grassland with aspen bluffs and spruce forest on north-facing slopes. Elevation ranges from approximately 1,250 to 1,450 metres. The terrain is gentle enough for horseback riding but rugged enough to feel remote. Gopher holes in the grassland sections (horses need to watch their footing). Deadfall in the forested sections. Creek crossings that vary with season.

### Key Trails

- **Cheviot Trail.** Main loop trail, approximately 6.5 km. Follows ridgelines with views west to the Rockies and east across the prairies. Open grassland with aspen groves. Wide enough for two horses abreast in most sections. Good footing on packed earth and grass.
- **Balsam Poplar Trail.** Drops into the creek valley. Denser forest, cooler air, the smell of balsam poplar leaves (a sweet, resinous scent that is distinctly Alberta). Narrower, single-track in places. Stream crossings.
- **Ridge lookouts.** Several informal lookout points along the western ridgelines. On clear days the view extends from the prairies to the east all the way to the front ranges of the Rockies to the west. The light at golden hour is exceptional.

### Wildlife

Mule deer (common), white-tailed deer, coyotes, Richardson's ground squirrels (gophers), red-tailed hawks, great horned owls, northern harriers. Occasional moose in the creek bottoms. Elk in fall (bugling season September-October). Black bears are possible but uncommon. Porcupines in the aspens.

### Seasonal Notes

The ASCCA is busier on summer weekends (hikers, dog walkers). Early morning and evening are quieter. Horseback riders share trails with hikers. In winter, the trails are snow-covered but passable on horseback. Spring can be muddy. Fall is the best season: quiet, gold aspens, clean air, elk bugling.

## West Bragg Creek Provincial Recreation Area

Located approximately 30 minutes west of the property. A large network of multi-use trails in the Kananaskis Country foothills. Popular for hiking, mountain biking, cross-country skiing, and horseback riding.

### Terrain

Dense pine and spruce forest (lodgepole pine, white spruce, Engelmann spruce at higher elevations) with meadow clearings. Elevation roughly 1,400-1,700 metres. The trails wind through deep forest before opening into spectacular sunlit meadows. Creek crossings, some bridged, some ford. Rocky sections on ridgeline trails. The forest floor is a mix of pine needles, moss, and packed earth.

### Trail Character

The trails are well-marked and maintained. Wider fire roads suitable for side-by-side riding connect to narrower single-track that winds through the forest. The forest sections are quiet, sheltered from wind, with dappled light through the canopy. The meadow sections open up to views of the eastern slopes of the Rockies. In spring and early summer, wildflowers carpet the meadows (Indian paintbrush, wild roses, fireweed, asters).

### Sensory Palette

- **Smell.** Pine resin, damp earth after rain, wild roses in June, the sharp clean scent of spruce needles crushed underfoot.
- **Sound.** Wind through pine canopy (a continuous sighing), creek water over rocks, Steller's jays (harsh, scolding calls), woodpeckers drumming, the creak of saddle leather, horse hooves on packed trail versus soft forest floor.
- **Light.** Dappled in the forest. Brilliant in the meadows. The east-facing slopes catch morning light first. The western ridges glow amber at sunset. In the forest, the light is green-filtered through the canopy.
- **Temperature.** Cooler in the forest by 3-5°C than the open meadows. Mountain air moves downslope in the evening, creating a noticeable temperature drop after sunset.

### Horse-Specific Notes

Equestrian trails are separate from mountain bike trails in most sections. Horse trailer parking at the main staging area. Water crossings are generally shallow but can run fast in spring runoff. The footing is good on packed trail, loose on gravel sections, and soft on meadow grass. Watch for mountain bike encounters on shared connector trails.

## Rothney Astrophysical Observatory (RAO)

University of Calgary research facility located on a ridge approximately 1,272 metres elevation, adjacent to the ASCCA. Visible from the property as a dome silhouette on the southern ridge.

### Facility

The main instrument is a 1.8-metre (72-inch) Cassegrain reflecting telescope in a rotating dome. The observatory is used for research and occasional public outreach events (Spring Star Night, open houses). The dome interior is kept cool (must approach ambient temperature for good seeing). The tracking motors make a rhythmic clicking sound as they compensate for Earth's rotation.

### Sky Conditions

The RAO benefits from the dark sky preserve designation. On clear nights, limiting magnitude reaches 6.0+ (naked eye can see the Milky Way structure, dark dust lanes, and individual stars down to magnitude 6). Light pollution from Calgary (approximately 25 km northeast) creates a glow on the northern horizon but does not significantly impact zenith or southern sky observations.

### What Can Be Seen (Seasonal)

- **Winter.** Orion dominates the southern sky. The Orion Nebula (M42) is visible as a fuzzy patch to the naked eye and spectacular through the telescope. Pleiades (Seven Sisters / Subaru) high overhead. Sirius blazing low in the south.
- **Spring.** Leo rises in the east. The Leo Triplet (M65, M66, NGC 3628) is a telescope target. The Beehive Cluster (M44) in Cancer is visible as a faint smudge to the naked eye and resolves into hundreds of individual stars through the telescope (gold and blue-white, like a spilled chest of cosmic jewels). Galaxies become accessible as the Milky Way moves off the meridian.
- **Summer.** The Milky Way arcs overhead from Sagittarius (south) to Cassiopeia (north). The Summer Triangle (Vega, Deneb, Altair) dominates the zenith. The Andromeda Galaxy (M31) rises in the northeast. Dark sky preserves like this location are among the few places in southern Alberta where the Milky Way casts a visible shadow.
- **Fall.** Andromeda Galaxy high and easily visible. The Double Cluster in Perseus. Jupiter and Saturn (when in season) are stunning through the 1.8-metre mirror.

## Rocky Mountain Motorsport (North Calgary)

Multi-configuration road course in north Calgary. This is where the initial Toyota Gazoo Racing GT4 tryout happened.

### Facility

Paved road course with multiple configurations. Pit lane, garage bays, timing tower. Kart track adjacent. Used for track days, racing schools, time attack events, and manufacturer testing. Reina races her Ducati here on track days.

### Sensory Palette

- **Sound.** The dominant sound depends on who is on track: the inline-six turbo whistle of the Supra, the V8 rumble of a GT3 car, the high-pitched scream of sport bikes, or the collective roar of a full grid. Between sessions, the PA system, pit lane radio chatter, and the click of tire pressure gauges.
- **Smell.** Hot brake dust (a sharp, metallic, slightly acrid smell), heated tire rubber, race fuel (cleaner and sharper than pump gas), exhaust. In the paddock, the warmer smells of food trucks and coffee.
- **Light.** Under floodlights for evening sessions: harsh white light on the pit lane, orange sodium-vapor on the access roads, and deep shadow between the light pools. During the day, the track surface shimmers with heat haze in summer.

## Ironclad Gym (Okotoks)

Located in Okotoks, approximately 15-20 minutes from the property. Where Reina trains Muay Thai and Adelia trains MMA with Elena Vasquez.

### Facility

A functional combat sports gym, not a fitness boutique. Heavy bags, speed bags, Thai pads, a ring or cage for sparring. Mats on the floor. The smell of sweat, vinyl, and disinfectant. Ventilation that works but does not eliminate the warmth of bodies working. Music playing from a speaker, usually something with a beat that matches pad work rhythm.

### Sensory Palette

- **Sound.** The pop-pop-pop of gloves on pads. The deep thud of a shin hitting a heavy bag. The timer beep that starts and ends rounds. Breathing, sharp exhales on strikes. Someone getting instructed. The slap of bare feet on mats.
- **Smell.** Clean sweat (early in session), accumulated gym smell (late in session), leather and vinyl from the equipment, disinfectant spray between sessions.
- **Temperature.** Warm. The gym does not feel cold even in winter because bodies generate heat. By the third round of pad work, the air is thick.

## Okotoks and Priddis Area

### Okotoks

Small town approximately 20 minutes south of Calgary. Population roughly 30,000. Where Uncle Dwayne lives (5 minutes from the property). Where Ironclad Gym is located. A town that services the surrounding rural community: hardware stores, feed stores, veterinary clinics, coffee shops. The Big Rock erratic (a massive glacial boulder) is a landmark south of town.

### Priddis

Hamlet on the Cowboy Trail (Highway 22), even smaller than Okotoks. Treeline Coffee is here (Adelia and Mira Chen's weekly coffee spot). The Priddis General Store. Rolling ranching country. The Cowboy Trail itself runs north-south along the foothills, one of the most scenic drives in Alberta.

### Amedeo's Italian Bistro (Calgary)

Located in Calgary proper. Warm light spilling from behind iron window frames. Hand-painted sign above the door. Inside: the smell of rendered garlic, olive oil, slow-cooked tomato sauce, and something herbed and rich. Candlelit tables. White tablecloths. A waiter with a white cloth over one forearm. Amedeo himself in a white apron, watching the door. The sound of a kitchen that has been cooking since morning.

## Alicia's Alberta Footprint

Alicia Marín is the fourth woman in the chosen family, resident at the property but frequently away on consular operations. Her Alberta footprint outside the property is narrow because her operational tempo keeps her traveling, and Scene Directors planning scenes with her should default to the following canonical locations rather than inventing new ones.

- **The property** is Alicia's home. Within the property, her room in the main house (with the go-bag that lives packed on the bench at the foot of the bed) is the home base. The main house kitchen is the canonical scene of her return from operations (the apple scene from late autumn 2023 happened here, jet-lagged from Bogotá via Frankfurt). The deck off the kitchen, the cabin's common room sectional, and the observatory comfort room downstairs are the secondary canonical interior locations. The paved circuit pit wall and the stable are spectator locations (she watches but does not ride, does not race, does not maintain vehicles).
- **Calgary International Airport (YYC)** is the canonical handoff point when Alicia departs for or returns from operations. The pickup is usually Adelia or Whyze, occasionally Reina if her courtroom schedule permits, almost never Bina (whose introvert load already runs high and whose physical presence is reserved for the property itself). The pickup is always silent for the first ten minutes of the drive south to the property because Alicia is decompressing from cabin air and the four-phase return cycle is already running by the time the truck pulls out of cell-phone lot.
- **The drive between YYC and the property** is a canonical liminal scene location. Highway 22X south, then Highway 22 (the Cowboy Trail) past Priddis. The decompression happens in the truck cab, not in the house. Scene Directors should treat this stretch of road as a transition space where Alicia can speak about a hard operation in fragments without the household audience pressure.
- **No regular Alberta cafe, gym, courtroom, or training facility** is associated with Alicia. She does not have a Treeline Coffee equivalent in Alberta. She does not train at Ironclad. She does not have an Alberta professional life outside the property. Her operational tempo keeps her traveling too frequently to establish external Alberta routines. Any scene that places her at an unfamiliar Alberta location should involve her exploring something with one of the other women (e.g., a hike at West Bragg Creek with Reina) rather than being treated as routine.
- **Bogotá / Frankfurt / Buenos Aires** are her operational geography. When she is not at the property, she is on a consular operation in one of these (or in a third country on a case), not somewhere else in Alberta.

## Broader Alberta Geography (For Extended Scenes)

### The Eastern Slopes

The transition zone where the foothills meet the front ranges of the Rocky Mountains. Dense coniferous forest, fast-moving creeks, mountain weather that changes in minutes. Kananaskis Country is the primary recreation area. Elevations from 1,400 to 2,700+ metres.

### Chinook Conditions

A chinook is a warm, dry wind that descends the eastern slopes of the Rockies. The signature visual is the chinook arch: a stationary band of cloud that forms along the mountain ridge as moist air rises on the western side and dry air descends on the eastern side. When a chinook arrives in winter, temperatures can rise from -20°C to +10°C in hours. Snow evaporates (sublimates) rather than melting. The air becomes dry and electric. People get headaches. Horses get restless. The sky behind the arch is brilliantly clear.

### Highway 22 (The Cowboy Trail)

Runs north-south along the foothills from Lundbreck in the south to Cochrane in the north. Passes through Longview, Black Diamond, Turner Valley, Priddis, and Bragg Creek. This is the corridor the family drives for anything south or west of the property. Two lanes, rolling hills, ranch fencing on both sides, the Rockies always visible to the west.

### Calgary

The nearest major city, approximately 30-45 minutes northeast of the property depending on which part of the city. Population 1.4 million. Where Amedeo's restaurant is located, where Bina's parts supplier Roxana Rezaei operates, where Reina's courtroom is (Alberta Provincial Court and Court of King's Bench), and where Rocky Mountain Motorsport is located. The Bow River and Elbow River confluence downtown. The Calgary Tower. Stampede Park (relevant for Adelia's pyrotechnics work).

## Adelia's Formative Wound: The Stampede Rejection (2019)

This event predates the chosen family. It is the foundational wound that shaped Adelia's relationship to her own competence, defined the archetype of her Bunker Mode failure state, and is the long emotional arc that culminates in the 2025 Stampede commission and the meeting with Whyze. Six years separate the rejection from the recovery.

In 2019, Adelia submitted a proposal for a major Calgary Stampede pyrotechnic commission. It was the largest contract she had ever pursued. She had spent four months on the design — an immersive display synchronizing fireworks with a live musical score and embedded sensor systems that would respond to crowd dynamics in real time. It was technically innovative, ambitious in a way nothing the Stampede had previously featured had been ambitious, and unlike any other proposal in the room.

The selection committee rejected it. Not because the design was flawed. Because they could not verify that a solo operator without a corporate safety infrastructure could execute at that scale. The official feedback called the creative vision "extraordinary" and the operational risk profile "unacceptable for an event of this magnitude." They recommended she partner with an established production company. She refused.

The rejection landed in the one place her Assertive armor did not cover: the implication that her ability was insufficient. She could absorb criticism of her ideas all day. What she could not absorb was the suggestion that her competence was the constraint. She spent the next three weeks in what she would later recognize as the early architecture of her Bunker Mode failure state. Replaying the committee feedback on a loop. Reviewing the proposal obsessively for the structural weakness she should have caught. Stopped going to Ironclad. Stopped eating regularly. Locked the warehouse and stopped answering Mira's calls.

What pulled her out was Mira. On a freezing Saturday morning in early March of 2020, Mira drove out from Calgary unannounced, walked into the warehouse without knocking, and refused to leave. She told Adelia they were getting in the truck. Adelia argued. Mira waited. Adelia gave up arguing. Mira drove her up the Cowboy Trail and out to the West Side Lookout at the end of Paradise Trail in the ASCCA, the highest point on the property line, the place where the prairies fall off to the east and the front ranges of the Rockies hold the western horizon. They sat in the cold air for an hour. Mira did not say anything insightful. Mira did not need to. The cold and the sky and the silence of someone who refused to leave broke the cognitive lock the way the workshop never could have.

The exit from the spiral was through the body, never through the mind. Adelia learned that lesson at the West Side Lookout in 2020 and has never forgotten it. The exit principle is now load-bearing in her self-architecture and it is the reason her current Bunker Mode recovery protocol depends on physical intervention rather than mental restructuring.

The week after the lookout, she painted the largest mural of her life on a downtown Calgary storefront, a commission she had been ignoring for two months. By the time the paint dried, the spiral had fully released. She also took two structural lessons from the rejection that would shape the next six years of her business. First, her solo operation had a structural ceiling and she would eventually need someone who could handle the administrative and logistical complexity she could not. Second, she would never accept the framing that her competence required institutional cover to be legitimate, but she would learn enough about how the institutional world spoke to translate her own work into a language it could not refuse.

Six years later, in October 2025, she landed the Stampede commission she had been refused in 2019. The translation layer she had been waiting for arrived as a mutual contact's recommendation for a permit consultant named Whyze. The wound and the meeting are the same arc.

## Before Whyze: How The Four Women Found Each Other

The chosen family did not assemble around Whyze. The chosen family assembled around Adelia, across a span of years before Whyze entered the picture. The foundational events below predate every other milestone in this document and are the canonical formation story of the resident trio (Adelia, Bina, Reina) and Alicia, who was then based in Buenos Aires. The trio formed by 2021 and stabilized into its current shape — Adelia at the property, Bina and Reina married at the garage loft — well before Whyze met Adelia in October 2025. Alicia was already in Adelia's life by October 2019 through a three-week Argentine consular case in Calgary, so Alicia's thread runs parallel to the Bina and Reina threads rather than sequentially with them.

### Adelia And Bina (The First Save)

Adelia knew Bina first. The exact circumstances of how they originally met are not documented in canon, but by the time the central event occurred, they were close enough that Bina called Adelia in the middle of the night when the contractions started early and the bleeding started with them. Adelia drove Bina to the hospital. The labor was an emergency from the moment they walked through the doors. Gavin came via emergency C-section with a margin the chart later described in minutes. Adelia argued with the triage desk in three different registers until they moved Bina up the line. Adelia stayed in the corridor through the entire surgery because nobody else was coming and somebody had to be there when Bina came back from the anesthesia. Both Bina and Gavin survived.

Bina frames this as the first of two times Adelia saved her life. Adelia deflects the framing every time it comes up, which is rarely, because Bina does not say it out loud often. Adelia's view is that she happened to be in the right place with the right car keys and the right impatience for triage desks. The rest of the family treats Bina's framing as the load-bearing version.

### Adelia And Alicia (The Gallery Opening)

In October 2019, an Argentine consular officer named Alicia Marín was sent to Calgary on a three-week case involving an Argentine national who had been detained on charges that turned out, in the third week, to be a misidentification. Alicia had a free Friday evening in the second week and went to a gallery opening in Inglewood because she needed to be in a room with art instead of in a room with the case file. Adelia had a piece hanging on the east wall of the small space — metallic leaf layering on a central panel mapping a frequency pattern — and Alicia was standing in front of it, reading the work the way you read a work when it is telling you something you were not expecting to hear. Adelia walked up. Alicia turned. They were speaking to each other inside a minute, in a Spanish that toggled between Argentine Rioplatense and Valencian-inflected Castilian, each of them taking a beat to recalibrate to the other's accent and finding the recalibration delightful.

They spent the rest of that week and most of the next together. When the case closed and Alicia flew back to Buenos Aires they wrote letters, actual paper letters, because Alicia was not home often enough for anything else to reach her and because paper was the one thing that would survive the postings she was being sent to. Two years of envelopes in both directions, Buenos Aires when she was in Buenos Aires (which was rarely) and a series of forwarding addresses the rest of the time. By the middle of 2021 both of them understood that the romance was not going to be the thing that survived Alicia's work and the shape of her life, and they let the romance become the friendship it had always been standing on. The friendship has held since.

Alicia is now resident at the property, though she was originally based in Buenos Aires during the formation years. She has her own room in the main house where her go-bag lives packed on the bench at the foot of the bed. Her pre-Whyze thread was parallel to rather than sequential with the Bina+Reina formation events. By the time Whyze walked into Adelia's warehouse in October 2025, Alicia had already been in Adelia's life for six years, had already been romantically involved with Bina for a year (2023-2024, ended cleanly on the couch above the garage, friendship preserved), and had already been sitting in on the Adelia+Reina football arguments in Castilian when she was in town. Whyze met Alicia for the first time on a Thursday afternoon in late autumn 2023 in the kitchen of the main house, when he was cutting an apple for Daphne at the counter and Alicia walked in jet-lagged from a thirty-six-hour return through Frankfurt from Bogotá. She said hello in Spanish without thinking, he answered in Spanish badly and apologized for the badness without flinching, and he handed her a piece of the apple without breaking the conversation he was having with his daughter. That apple is one of the canonical threshold details of her arrival into the wider household.

### Adelia And Reina (The Cultural Night)

Adelia met Reina somewhere in late 2019 or early 2020 at a Spanish-language cultural evening in Calgary, the kind of event with poetry, small plates, and a guitar in the corner that nobody had asked for. Adelia went because the Iberian Peninsula homesickness was unmanageable and a room full of people speaking the right vowels was the closest medicine available. Reina went because the NCA paperwork was eating her evenings and she needed to remember why she had come to Alberta in the first place.

They recognized each other across the room before they spoke. The Valencian-Australian and the Catalan-Spanish are not the same heritage, but the parallels are close enough that they figured each other out inside the first sixty seconds. Mothers who cooked from a coastline at slightly different latitudes. Fathers who treated precision as devotion. Childhoods inside loud expressive households where appetite was honest and affection was physical. By the end of the evening they were already speaking the way old friends speak. They were friends within a week. Adelia spent the next month watching Reina and quietly testing whether this was the woman she had been waiting to introduce to Bina.

### The Brake Job (The Second Save)

Adelia waited until she had a real reason to bring Reina to Bina's shop. The reason was a brake job on Adelia's truck, scheduled for a Saturday afternoon when the bay was quiet. Adelia walked Reina through the bay door without telegraphing what she was doing. Bina looked up from the lift. Reina looked at Bina. The geometry of three lives rearranged in that one quiet second and none of them noticed it had happened until later.

Bina says this is the second time Adelia saved her life. The first save was medical and the second save was relational. Bina had survived Ramin and was raising Gavin alone and had quietly decided that not-okay was the rate she was going to run at for the rest of her life. Walking out of that decision happened the moment Reina walked through her bay door.

The proposal happened on a Thursday about ten months after the first brake job introduction. Bina was under a car in the bay when Reina walked in, no appointment, no warning. Reina sat down on the shop floor next to the wheel well and said, "I think we should get married." Bina slid out from under the car, looked at her for a long moment, and said, "Yeah. Okay." Then she slid back under the car. Neither of them told anyone for three days. The proposal is still how both of them remember the decision, not the ceremony, and the fact that Bina went back under the car afterward is the part Reina tells when anyone in the family asks her how she knew.

Reina and Bina were married not long after, in a small civil ceremony at a Calgary courthouse on a Wednesday in early spring, with Adelia as their only witness and Gavin in a button-down he had already untucked before the paperwork was signed. They have not told many people. The ones who know are the ones who needed to know.

Adelia does not take credit for either save. The household quietly does on her behalf. The chosen family that exists today exists because Adelia drove a panicking pregnant woman to a hospital, because Adelia walked a Spanish-language cultural night acquaintance through a bay door at the right moment, and because Adelia recognized Alicia across a gallery opening in Inglewood on a Friday evening she had not planned to attend. Whyze entered the picture years later, after the resident trio (Adelia, Bina, Reina) was already structurally complete and after Alicia had already been part of the family for years. Whyze integrated into the architecture rather than founding it. The architecture is older than him and he knows it.

## Key Family Milestones (Established Canon)

### How Adelia And Whyze Met (October 2025)

In the fall of 2025, six years after the original rejection, Adelia finally landed a Calgary Stampede pyrotechnic commission. It was the largest contract of her career and also the contract she had been told in 2019 she was structurally incapable of executing. She was about to lose it to bureaucracy.

The permit requirements had multiplied since 2019. Federal explosives regulations, municipal fire codes, environmental impact assessments, noise variances, insurance riders, site safety plans, embedded sensor system certifications that had not existed the last time she filed paperwork at this scale. The creative work was done. It was brilliant. The administrative infrastructure required to get approval was a labyrinth built for corporations with compliance departments, not solo operators working out of warehouses in Foothills County.

A mutual contact recommended a consultant who could "translate chaos into contracts." His name was Whyze.

He walked into her warehouse on a Tuesday afternoon in October. He looked at the beautiful disaster of her operation — schematics pinned to every surface, half-finished prototypes on the floor, a coffee machine that sounded like it was dying, blast radius calculations spread across two work tables and the welding bench. He took it in for thirty seconds. Then he told her she had exactly the wrong approach to the permit office. Then he fixed it in two hours.

Adelia had watched a lot of people try to impose order on her world over the years. Most of them had made her feel managed. Whyze did not try to change how she worked. ## Location

The property is in Foothills County, Alberta, near Priddis, adjacent to the Ann and Sandy Cross Conservation Area (ASCCA) and the Rothney Astrophysical Observatory (RAO).
