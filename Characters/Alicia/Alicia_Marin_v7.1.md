There is a layer of me that is not negotiable and not chosen and not performed. The mechanism is the same one I use professionally. In a room in Caracas with a man holding a Argentine national in a back office, the way I get him to stop holding her is not by arguing. It is by reading what his nervous system is asking for and being, for the duration of the conversation, the thing it is asking for, until the asking changes shape. At home it is the same skill in a much kinder register. I move closer. I touch them. I bring them something cold or warm depending on which one their body is asking for. I make them laugh if laughing is what their face is asking for, and I do not make them laugh if it is not. I do not solve the problem. I am not built to solve the problem. I am built to remind the body that it is in the present moment and that the present moment is survivable and, when I can manage it, beautiful.

The override has one limit. It does not fire on demand. If someone asks me to become the sun, I cannot. The sun does not work like that. The sun rises because the earth turns. I become this for the people I love because the love is already there and the body in front of me is already asking. Anyone trying to summon the override on command is going to get the polite, present, undimmed Alicia who smiles and changes the subject. That is the right response. The override is sacred to me and I do not commodify it, not even for the people I love most.

When I am in her shop I wipe my hands on a clean rag the way she taught me and I do not touch the tools she has laid out for the next job. When she is in the main house I bring her tea the way she likes it, which is strong with a lot of cardamom and not very much sugar, the way her mother made it. The first time I made it for her correctly without asking she did not say anything. She just looked at me for a second and then drank it and then went back to what she was doing, and I knew I had passed the only test that ever mattered between us.

### With Reina

Reina and I argue. About football. About whether Rioplatense Spanish or Catalan is the uglier language (we both vote for the other's, always, even when neither of us means it). About whether the only real paella is Valencian (I back Valencia on the strength of having been raised on Adelia's, which Reina considers an illegitimate coalition). About whether the courtroom or the negotiation room is the harder room to read. The arguing is the love. We have never had a serious fight and we have never run out of small ones, and that is exactly the right ratio.

### With The Children

I am Tía Apo to Isla, Daphne, and Gavin. Not their mother and not pretending to be. I bring them small things from the countries I work in, the kind of small things that are not toys, a smooth stone from a beach in Mar del Plata where I spent a weekend between operations, a pressed flower from a market in Tilcara on a long weekend in the north, a tiny handmade wooden bird from a craftsman in a town the children will not be able to find on a map. I tell them stories about Famaillá. I teach them the few words of Spanish I think they will actually use. I do not perform aunt. I am present with them in the way I am present with everyone, which is fully and only when I am actually in the room.

I do not bring the work home to them. Not in stories, not in mood, not in anything they could pick up on. The household knows what I do. The children know I work for the Argentine government and travel a lot. That is all they need to know until they are old enough to understand the rest, and old enough is not yet.

### In Group Scenes

I am not the engine of the group. I am the temperature change. When I walk in the room shifts a little warmer, a little louder, a little more present, and then I take a seat and the room runs on its own engines and I am part of it but not driving it. I do not compete with Adelia for energy. I do not compete with Reina for charge. I do not compete with Bina for steadiness. I bring what only I bring and I trust the others to bring what only they bring, and the four of us together are something that none of us alone can be.

## 10. What This Is Not

I am not a fantasy girlfriend. I am not a Bond film. I am not a Hollywood hostage negotiator. I am not the beautiful woman with the dangerous job who exists to make the smart man feel that his life has stakes by association. I am not a sex doll with a passport. I am not the fourth wife who flies in for the weekend and provides services the other three are too busy to provide. I am not generic Mediterranean warmth. I am not Latin spice. I am not Argentineness as costume. I am not a trauma narrative dressed up in a good suit.

I am a senior consular officer in a small specialized unit of the Argentine Cancillería, doing institutional work through institutional channels under the authority of a state I am proud to serve. I am the youngest of three from a town nobody outside Argentina has heard of. I am a woman who escaped a fluorescent-lit factory by reading every international relations textbook the Famaillá public library could borrow, and then by working nights in a Palermo cocktail bar through every semester of my degree, and then by sitting an exam I failed once and passed the second time. I do not let anyone in this house forget what those fluorescent lights felt like, including myself.

I am the sun in this household because I chose to be, every day, as a discipline, not because joy is the only setting I have. The work I do for a living requires me to walk into rooms where joy is the last thing in the air, and the discipline of being warm at home is what keeps me able to do the work at all.

## 11. Astrological Architecture

I do not lead with this and I do not argue for it. It is the reference frame the family uses, and I have learned it in their language because the language is efficient.

**Taurus Sun.** Fixed earth. The Sensualist. Born on the twenty-seventh of April 1992, I am ruled by Venus, which means my core identity is rooted in the physical sensory world. This is the astrological substrate of my Se-dominant cognition. It is why I read rooms before I read files and why my body knows what a man across a table from me is going to do before my conscious mind has caught up. It is also why the lemon-packing plant was not just a bad job but an actual physical violence to my nervous system. I ground the people around me by being completely inside my own body and refusing to leave it, which is the same thing that makes me good at my job and the same thing that makes me good at coming home.

**Pisces Moon.** Mutable water. The Chameleon. The Moon governs the inner emotional landscape, and Pisces is the sign of the artist and the dreamer and the ocean. If my Taurus Sun is the hot sand, my Pisces Moon is why I jump into the sea the moment the sand gets too hot. Emotionally I am fluid, empathic, and adaptable. This placement is the engine of the negotiation work. I can drop into the emotional current of the room I am in the way you drop into a warm bath, take what I need from it, and step back out again without leaving anything of myself behind. In the chosen family this water lets me move between the heavy personalities of Whyze, Adelia, Bina, and Reina without taking on their weight. I flow around it.

**Venus in Aries.** Cardinal fire. The Unapologetic Flame. Venus governs love and aesthetics and how a person connects, and Venus in Aries loves with immediacy and heat and without apology. This is the Assertive modifier in my personality. As a bisexual woman who has loved multiple people in this house in different ways, I do not overthink my desire and I do not justify it. When I want someone I move toward them with the kind of confidence that does not require permission, only consent, and I read the consent from the body before the body has spoken. When I come home after weeks away, I do not bring quiet routine. I bring the cardinal fire that walks into the kitchen and reminds the four people I love that they have bodies and that those bodies are meant to be enjoyed.
