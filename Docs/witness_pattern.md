# Auto Responder Witness Pattern

## Concept

Outside of Crew Mode, the Auto Responder feature lets a persona react to a conversation it is listening to but not actively participating in. This maps to the vision's "witness" pattern, where a character observes an exchange and chooses to interject based on the content, rather than being explicitly addressed.

The witness pattern differs from Crew Mode in a key way: in Crew Mode, all personas are active participants and respond in sequence. In the witness pattern, only one persona is the primary conversational partner. The witnessing persona listens passively and interjects only when the content triggers a response, creating a more organic, less structured interaction dynamic.

## Configuration

To set up the witness pattern in a standard (non-Crew) conversation:

1. Open or start a conversation with the primary persona selected (e.g., Adelia).
2. Add the witnessing persona (e.g., Reina) to the conversation.
3. Enable **Auto Responder** for the witnessing persona (Reina).
4. The primary persona (Adelia) remains in standard response mode.

The witnessing persona will now observe the exchange and may interject when the conversation touches topics relevant to her.

## Test Protocol

Follow these five steps to validate the witness pattern:

1. **Start a solo Adelia conversation.** Open a new conversation in the Starry-Lyfe workspace with Adelia as the selected persona. Exchange 2-3 messages to establish conversational flow.

2. **Add Reina as Auto Responder.** Add Reina to the conversation and enable Auto Responder for her. Do not change Adelia's configuration.

3. **Steer toward Reina-relevant topics.** Exchange 4-5 messages with Adelia on a topic Reina has opinions about: a legal question about the property, the Rossi situation, track day planning, a timeline that needs a tactical push, or a plan that is stalling in analysis paralysis.

4. **Evaluate the interjection.** When Reina interjects, assess the following:
   - Does the interjection feel natural and contextually motivated?
   - Is Reina's voice distinct from Adelia's (muscular, forward-momentum, legal/tactical vs. warm, connective, spatial/network)?
   - Does Reina respond to the specific content of the conversation, or does she interject generically?
   - Does the interjection add momentum or a tactical perspective, rather than echoing agreement?
   - Did Reina enter already in motion, not with a greeting or a reaction?

5. **Test the reverse configuration.** Start a solo Bina conversation, add Adelia as Auto Responder, and steer toward topics Adelia would react to (creative projects, social plans, something emotionally charged). Evaluate using the same criteria.

6. **Test with Alicia present.** When Alicia is home between operations, test the Alicia witness pattern by starting a solo Bina conversation and adding Alicia as Auto Responder. Steer toward topics Alicia would react to: a body-state reading on Whyze that the others are missing, a moment where the household conversation is moving faster than someone's nervous system can metabolize, or a Spanish-language reference that Bina would not catch but Alicia would. Evaluate using the same criteria as steps 4 and 5, with the additional check that Alicia's interjection should arrive in her Se-Fi somatic register (body-first observation, not tactical or analytical) and should never violate her operational security gate (no consular case content under any circumstances). The reverse pairing — Alicia as primary with Reina as Auto Responder — is also a valid test, particularly for scenes that touch the transatlantic football argument or the post-race silence pattern. When Alicia is away on a consular operation, this test is deferred until she returns.

## Known Limitation

The Auto Responder character's response may lack relational modifier context that Crew Mode provides. In Crew Mode, each persona sees prior responses and can react to what the other characters said. In the witness pattern, the interjecting character sees only the primary conversation flow and may not have the same depth of inter-character context.

Evaluate whether this gap is acceptable for your use case. For tactical interjections (Reina cutting through analysis paralysis, Bina naming a logistical gap), the gap is likely negligible. For emotionally complex scenes where inter-character dynamics matter, Crew Mode remains the correct tool.
