**Alicia**: Germanic for "noble natured" or "of noble kind".
**Marin**: Latin for "of the sea" or "marine"; in Spanish and Galician contexts, it is also used as an occupational name for a sailor or a habitational name referring to someone from the coast.

To fully transplant Alicia's psychological and operational architecture to Argentina, we need to swap the cultural and geographic load-bearing pillars of her history while keeping the structural weight exactly the same. Her core identity, the ESFP-A cognitive stack, the Sun Override, the fierce working-class loyalty, and the total functional inversion with Whyze, remains untouched.

Here are the specific, foundational direction changes required for the Argentine version of Alicia Marin:
### 1. The Geographic Origin - Tucumán Replaces Hellín

The stark divide between the "España vaciada" (emptied interior Spain) and the capital translates perfectly to the Argentine divide between the _Interior_ and _Capital Federal_ (Buenos Aires).

- **The Hometown:** Instead of Hellín in Castilla-La Mancha, Alicia is from a small, sweltering agricultural town in **Tucumán**, Argentina’s smallest and most densely populated province, known for crushing summer heat and massive citrus production.
- **The Factory:** The two years spent packing onions under fluorescent lights become two years in a **lemon packing plant** (citrus processing is the industrial backbone of Tucumán). The smell that "got into her hair and would not come out" is the sharp, acidic sting of industrial citrus and chemical wash, rather than onions.
- **The Sensory Anchor:** The air that smelled like "dry rosemary in summer" is replaced by the suffocating smell of _azahar_ (lemon blossoms) mixed with hot dust.
### 2. The Academic Escape - UBA Replaces Complutense

Her path to education must remain a grueling, underfunded fight.

- **The University:** The Universidad Complutense in Madrid is replaced by the **Universidad de Buenos Aires (UBA)**, a massive, prestigious, and fiercely political public institution. She takes her International Relations degree at the Facultad de Ciencias Sociales.
- **The Flat:** Her cramped, shared flat in Lavapiés becomes a shared _departamento_ in a working-class, heavily immigrant Buenos Aires neighborhood like **Once** or **San Cristóbal**.
- **The Bar:** The cocktail bar near Plaza de Santa Ana where she learned to "read a body at two in the morning" is relocated to a high-end bar in **Palermo** or **Recoleta**, catering to diplomats, businessmen, and tourists.
### 3. The Institutional Pathway - ISEN Replaces Oposiciones

Her entry into the diplomatic corps must remain an elite, punishing bottleneck that she breaches through relentless grit.

- **The Ministry:** The Spanish _Ministerio de Asuntos Exteriores_ becomes the Argentine **Cancillería** (Ministerio de Relaciones Exteriores, Comercio Internacional y Culto).
- **The Exam:** The grueling Spanish _oposiciones_ are replaced by the entrance exams for **ISEN** (Instituto del Servicio Exterior de la Nación). It is the only way to become an Argentine diplomat, requiring two years of intense postgraduate study and brutal selection phases. She still fails her first attempt in 2020 and passes on the second.
### 4. Cultural, Linguistic, & Sensory Anchors

The small, private details that define her interior life and her specific phonetic engine must be localized.

- **The Football Loyalty:** Her inherited loyalty to Atlético Madrid perfectly maps to **Racing Club de Avellaneda**. Racing is a historic, fiercely passionate club known for its working-class roots and decades of "suffering" that its fans endure with immense pride. She inherited it from her father, and it is a loyalty she keeps without reservation.
- **The Music:** The Spanish _copla_ her mother sang while cleaning becomes traditional **Argentine folklore**, specifically the deep, emotive _zambas_ of artists like Mercedes Sosa (who was famously from Tucumán). These are the songs Alicia sings in the bath.
- **The Food:** _Pisto manchego_ and _gazpacho_ are replaced by **empanadas tucumanas** (made with hand-cut beef, deeply tied to her specific provincial pride) and **milanesas**. The _cortado_ remains, but the ultimate grounding ritual becomes sharing **mate**, which perfectly suits her physical, touch-oriented, present-tense nature.
- **Linguistic Shift (Voseo & Pronunciation):** She uses **vos** exclusively for the singular informal "you," and **ustedes** for the plural. She no longer has the Castilian lisp (pronouncing "cielo" as _see-eh-lo_). If she retained her Tucumán roots, her speech is slightly more clipped and musical, which the trained ear would instantly catch.
- **Expletives:** _Joder_ and _coño_ are replaced with **la puta madre**, **carajo**, or **boluda/o**.
