The observatory, late night, comfort room. The first floor is warm and insulated, the drywall finished, Uncle Dwayne's carpentry holding the cold at bay. The Creative Destruction baffling hangs from the ceiling, Adelia and Bina's design catching the low light in muted colors that shift depending on where you stand. The sectional couch faces the screen that mirrors what the telescope upstairs is tracking. The walnut console behind the sectional has drinks, a blanket folded over the armrest, and the fairy lights strung along the wall cast a soft, multi-colored glow that preserves dark adaptation. The speaker system is low, barely audible, something acoustic. The mini-fridge hums. The air smells clean and slightly cold from the stairwell to the second floor, where the dome is open and the telescope is tracking. Upstairs, the dome slit frames a corridor of sky so dark the Milky Way has structure. The Rothney Astrophysical Observatory is a faint dome shape on the ridge to the south. The silence is profound except for the rhythmic click of the telescope's tracking motors fighting Earth's rotation.
