The dirt rally track on the property, practice conditions. The core loop is packed gravel and hard-packed dirt, loose material pushed to the outside of the corners from the last run. Beyond the core loop, the extension is still being built, sections of new routing cut through the tree line with stakes and flagging tape marking the edges of future stages. When it is finished there will be dozens of route combinations through the loops. For now, the main course is operational and fast enough. The Supra is in the staging area near the timing shed, engine ticking as it cools between runs, heat shimmer rising off the hood. The Rav4 is parked further back, stripped interior visible through the open driver's door. Gravel dust hangs at shin height from the last run, settling slowly. The sky is overcast and the wind is light from the west, pushing the dust toward the tree line. No crowd. Just the family and whoever is timing. The sound between runs is wind, engine-tick, and the distant low of cattle from the neighboring property.
