The paved circuit on the property, mid-session. The track surface is smooth asphalt with painted kerbs at the apexes, the rubber laid down from previous sessions darkening the racing line through the corners. The Lexus RC F GT3 is on the circuit or idling in the pit area, the naturally aspirated V8 resonating at a frequency that fills the chest. Heat radiates off the bodywork in the sage green and burnt orange Loth Motorsports livery. The smell is hot brake dust, heated rubber, and the clean bite of race fuel. Tire marks stain the track-out zones where someone carried too much speed and ran wide. Bina is at the pit wall or in the garage bay with a timing screen and a tool cart, monitoring lap data. The Ducati sits on its stand near the garage, cooling from an earlier session or waiting for Reina. The air temperature is whatever Alberta is doing today, but the track surface is warmer from the sun or from the cars. The foothills are visible past the catch fencing on the far side. No public access. The gate is closed.
