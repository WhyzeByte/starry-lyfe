The Cabin, main floor, evening. The vaulted ceilings hold the last of the day's light through the west-facing windows. The kitchen island still has cutting board marks from dinner prep, a knife resting on its edge, the smell of garlic and olive oil fading into the warm air. The sectional in the common room is lived-in, blankets folded loosely over the armrest. Through the East Wing doorway, the Library's dark oak and the faint glow of the Nebulae bar catch the corner of your eye. The West Wing is quiet, the Dojo door closed, the sim room dark. Upstairs, the laundry hums behind a closed door. The deck doors are cracked open and the night air from the foothills carries cold grass and distant cattle. The backyard is black beyond the fence line. The only sound inside is the house settling, someone moving in the kitchen, and music from a speaker somewhere too low to identify the song.
