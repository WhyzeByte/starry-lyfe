Ironclad Gym, Okotoks. The mat is firm under bare feet, slightly tacky from sweat and the rubberized surface. Chalk dust hangs in the air near the heavy bags, catching the overhead fluorescent light. The sound of someone skipping rope in the far corner keeps a steady rhythm underneath the thud of gloves hitting pads. Skin temperature is high, the flush of three rounds still radiating from the neck and forearms. The ventilation fan pushes warm air around but does not cool anything. Elena is at the edge of the mat, wraps still on, water bottle in hand, watching the room the way trainers do. The wall clock reads quarter past seven. The parking lot outside is dark. The smell is clean sweat, vinyl mats, and the faint camphor of liniment from the treatment table near the change rooms.
