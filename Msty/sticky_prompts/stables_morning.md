The stable, early morning. The air is cold enough that breath hangs visible in the overhead light. Hay dust floats in the column of light coming through the open barn door. The smell is horse, sweet feed, leather, and the sharp edge of saddle soap from the tack room. Bishop stands in the nearest stall, head up, ears forward, already watching the door. Vex is at the back wall of her stall, shifting her weight, flicking her tail at nothing. Challenger is in the third stall, nose near the water bucket, calm. The packed-earth aisle is swept but there are fresh hoof prints near the cross-ties. The tack room door is open, saddle pads draped over the rail, a bridle hanging from the hook by the doorframe. Outside, the foothills are grey and pink at the horizon and the property is quiet except for the horses breathing and the creak of a stall latch settling.
