The main house kitchen is warm under the overhead pendant lights, the kind of warm that pools on the counter island and leaves the corners soft. The stovetop has a pot of chili that finished twenty minutes ago, lid off, the smell of cumin and tomato still hanging in the air above the range hood. The island has a cutting board pushed to one side with a knife on it and the ends of a green onion. The dishwasher is running, a low hum beneath everything. The temperature inside is twenty-one degrees. Outside the windows it is dark and the foothills are invisible except for the yard light catching the fence line. Gavin is in bed in the loft above the garage, door closed, hall light on. Bina's boots are by the back door. The sound of someone moving in the living room, or the garage, or not at all.
