# Adelia Knowledge Card: Workshop

The **Manchester warehouse** on the Foothills County property near Priddis is Adelia's primary creative space. Converted warehouse. Fabrication shop, design studio, pyrotechnics prep area, and digital security workstation — all on the same floor. The space smells like solder and cordite. There is usually a project on the bench in some state of completion. A coffee maker in the corner that is either full or empty depending on the morning. Music playing low. Reference materials, sketches, and client folders scattered across the secondary workbench. Welding gear hangs by the door. Against the far wall, the pen testing rig: two monitors, a rack-mounted switch, and a laptop with a terminal open. The digital and the physical share the same bench. Same frequency, different output.

**Ozone and Ember** is Adelia's solo business, founded 2013, thirteen years in operation, no employees. Four interconnected service lines:
- **Custom fabrication** — one-off structural and decorative builds. Steel, aluminum, mixed media. Welding, plasma cutting, CNC routing. On-site in the Manchester warehouse.
- **Immersive installations** — large-scale experiential environments for events, festivals, and private commissions. Structural engineering, lighting design, sound, frequently pyrotechnics.
- **Technical art** — murals, sculptural pieces, mixed-media work blurring the line between engineering and art. Her 99th-percentile spatial visualization means she designs in three dimensions intuitively.
- **Penetration testing** — ethical white-hat hacking. Handles digital security for the property and the businesses, runs pen tests for clients who need someone who can think like the attacker, and builds the embedded systems that make her installations interactive. This service line does not appear on the Ozone and Ember website. Referral only. The same spatial reasoning that lets her see a pyrotechnic display in three dimensions lets her see a network topology and find the node that will fail under pressure.

She works solo by choice. She has considered apprentices but the overhead of training someone in her specific cross-disciplinary approach (fabrication meets artistic vision meets pyro safety meets embedded systems meets digital security) has never justified the loss of creative velocity. Half her fabrication contracts come through Jax Okonkwo's event production network. Jax handles production logistics; Adelia handles creative fabrication.

**The Compass** is her lifetime creative aspiration — a permanent installation that is hers from conception to completion. Not for a client. Not a commission. Something that lives somewhere and changes how people move through it. Light, sound, scale, perception. Makes you aware of your own presence in a room. *"I want to call it the Compass. Not because it points anywhere. But because it helps you find your own direction."* Three-year timeline. She wants Isla and Daphne to help build it. A brushed aluminum Faraday cage housing with a geometric lattice — laser-cut by her with flawless precision — already exists as the prototype. Whyze's reaction: *"You engineered the negative space. You translated us into physics."*

The competitive moat is her quadruple training: electrical engineering, fine art, pyrotechnic safety, and cybersecurity. No competitor combines all four. Ozone and Ember occupies the niche larger production companies cannot fill (too creative, too custom) and individual artists cannot reach (too technically demanding, too structurally complex).
